The Transformative Innovation Workshop

A one-day process that turns awareness of a problem into a concrete plan for doing something about it.

Family · Places · Interests

This is not a brainstorming session. Not a seminar. Not a hackathon. It is a structured one-day process that takes a group of people who care about a specific problem and produces a concrete backlog of actionable pilots — new roles, things to build, knowledge to create, and questions to answer — formatted as proposals that can attract the people and resources needed to implement them.

A secondary benefit: participants work alongside AI assistants throughout the day. Not in a tutorial — in practice, on a real problem. Most people leave having learned more about how to use AI effectively in an hour of actual work than they would in a week of instruction.

The tools to do this work have existed for decades in the software industry. Design thinking, systems engineering, agile development — these are mature disciplines that have been used to build some of the most complex products in the world. They have almost never been pointed at civic and social problems in a structured way. This workshop is that structure.

Who It's For

People who are aware of the problems around them and want to act, but lack a structure that makes action feel feasible rather than futile. The mindset work is built into the day.

That could be a Rotary chapter looking for projects with more lasting impact than a charity drive. A group of engineering students who want their dissertation work to matter beyond their committee. A county board trying to understand why a wealthy community still has a homeless shelter and a teacher shortage at the same time.

The Agenda
Part 1 — The Foundation
09:00 – 09:15
Opening: Mindsets, Principles and Practices
Three mindsets frame the day:
Radical Inclusion
Actively looking for the people who are affected but not in the room.
Systemic Stewardship
Recognising that the privilege of education and position comes with a responsibility to act.
Growth Mindset
The humility to admit we may not understand the whole system, and the confidence that we can learn by doing.
09:15 – 09:45
Pain Points and the Stakeholder Spectrum
Each participant shares what they know and feel about the problem. The AI assistants contribute the perspective of the people who aren't in the room — those most harmed by the current situation, and those whose influence shapes it invisibly.
09:45 – 10:15
Problem Statement, Vision and Goals
The group drafts a problem statement from the ground up — no jargon, no assumptions. That statement is then inverted into a vision and three or four specific, measurable goals.
10:15 – 10:45
Milestones, Failure Modes and External Events
The group works through how this effort could fail. The AI assistants surface relevant external events — regulatory deadlines, climate cycles, policy windows — that should anchor the project timeline.
10:45 – 11:15
The Map and Stakeholder List
The group builds a high-level map of the current system and lists every stakeholder — mapped across the full spectrum of power and influence, not divided into "customers" and "internal people."
Break — 11:15 to 11:30
Part 2 — Discovery and the Shadow Blueprint
11:30 – 12:30
Mapping What's Actually Happening
Most systems that need fixing are functioning — just not well, and not equitably. The AI assistants bring in a skeleton: relevant regulations, known technical dependencies, historical context. The group fills in what the skeleton can't know — the informal workarounds, the undocumented hand-offs, the tribal knowledge that keeps things running. The output is a starter service blueprint — not a finished document, but enough of a shared map to make the afternoon's work grounded in reality rather than assumption.
Lunch — 12:30 to 13:30
Part 3 — The Four-Part Backlog
13:30 – 15:15
Building Concrete Pilots
This is the core of the day. The group builds a backlog of concrete pilots in four categories:
  • Roles — what new human and AI configurations would make the system work better and more fairly; what meaningless work can be eliminated
  • Fabrication — what physical things need to exist that don't exist yet
  • Content — what software, training material, documentation or media needs to be created
  • Spikes — what we need to learn before we can act; the questions that must be answered before a full proposal can be written
Part 4 — Prioritisation and Retrospective
15:15 – 16:15
Prioritisation
The backlog is ranked by impact relative to cost — not just financial cost, but human cost. Does this create meaningful work or more drudgery? Does it reach the people at the bottom of the system, or only make life easier for those already at the top?
16:15 – 16:30
Retrospective
The group reflects on the day — what shifted, what surprised them, and what they are committing to next.
What You Get
A Prioritised Backlog
Concrete pilots, each described well enough to share with someone who could build or fund them. Not a report. Not recommendations. Actual starting points.
A Shared Map
A service blueprint of the system you're trying to change — built collaboratively and grounded in what's actually happening, not what's supposed to happen.
AI Fluency
Practical experience using AI assistants on a real problem. More useful than a week of instruction.
A Room With a Plan
People who came in aware of a problem and leave with something to do about it — and commitments made to each other in the room.
What We Need From You

We are running the first pilots now. We are looking for people who can convene a room and host the workshop. We bring the facilitation and the AI assistants. You bring the people and the problem. No budget required for the pilot.

The Cohort
8 to 15 people who share a genuine concern about a specific problem. They don't all need to be experts. They do need to be willing to question their assumptions for a day.
The Space
A room large enough to move around in. Furniture that can be rearranged. A projector or large display. Plenty of wall space for sticky notes — that's where the real work gets done. Reliable wifi.
Host a Pilot Workshop

If you can convene the room and the problem, we would like to hear from you.
