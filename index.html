Bruges, Belgium · April 14, 2005

Welcome. I began life in Meerut, a small academic and military town in northern India, where our house at 71 Western Kutchery Road has always been — and remains — Home.

I studied at St. Mary's Academy through high school, then spent four transformative years at the Regional Engineering College in Rourkela, graduating in Computer Science in 1992. More friends were made there than anywhere else in my life.

After college I joined CMC Limited in Calcutta, working on an extraordinary project to automate the steelmaking process at Bhilai Steel Plant — one of the largest steel factories in the world. CMC's office at 28 Camac Street was the focal point of the most intense friendships, the finest examples of human virtue and vice, and some of the most hilarious incidents of my professional life.

I moved to Calcutta proper in 1995, spending two rich years delving into the city's remarkable history and culture before coming to the United States in 1996. I have lived in the Washington DC area since, most recently in Ashburn, Virginia.

In 2002, after eight years away from academia, I completed a Master of Science in Computer Science at The George Washington University — primarily, I confess, for managing to finish my coursework at the eleventh hour.

I have been married since 1997, and am the proud father of two grown children.
