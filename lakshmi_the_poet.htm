Lakshmi the Poet

In which Tagore meets Linux — a brief lapse of Lakshmi's normal self.

Family · Places · Interests

In what Lakshmi calls a brief lapse of his normal self, he wrote the following version of Tagore's "Where the Mind is Without Fear" — publishable now that the copyrights on Tagore's works have lapsed.

After Tagore · Version 2.0

"Where the disk is without junk and the monitor is held high/
Where the OS is free/
Where the internet has not been broken up into {impregnable} domains/
By narrow corporate {firewalls}/
Where commands come out from the simplicity of the prompt/
Where tireless context switching stretches its arms towards the ultimate job scheduling/
Where the clear flow of {logic} has not lost its way/
Into the buggy code of Windows/
Where the computer is led forward by Thee/
Into ever-widening downloads and {installs}/
Into that heaven of Linux, my Father, let all computers awake."

© Lakshminarayan "Satyan" Chidambaran · January 26, 2002

Lakshmi tells me that this is version 2.0 — he did send me the beta, which he calls 1.0 — and has also dedicated this to me (thank you, Lakshmi!).
