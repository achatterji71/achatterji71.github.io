News & Radio

My reading habits have shifted considerably since 2005 — a reflection less of my changing tastes than of the changing nature of journalism itself. I dropped The Times of India and, more recently, the Washington Post, both long-time staples. This reflects what I can only describe as the global sellout of news outlets to corporate interests. The Statesman, once a great paper, is now a ghost of its storied past. All India Radio has lost its independence and sounds rather like Pravda these days.

What remains, and what I have added, are outlets I still trust.

The Hindu
The New York Times
The Telegraph — still my source for all things Calcutta
BBC News
National Public Radio

Philately & Numismatics

These two hobbies have been with me since childhood and show no signs of leaving. There is something grounding about holding a piece of paper or metal that has passed through history. Stamps and coins are, in their own way, the most democratic of archives.

Travel & Food
